Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about sealing your Nevada criminal record — answered by attorney Kenneth A. Stover.

How long does the record sealing process take in Nevada?

It depends on the offense. Dismissed charges or acquittals typically take 3–6 months. Misdemeanors usually take 6–12 months. Felony cases can take longer depending on complexity. We keep you informed every step of the way.

What are the waiting periods before I can apply?

After completing all sentence requirements (probation, fines, restitution), Nevada requires: immediately for dismissed charges or acquittals; 1 year for most misdemeanors; 2 years for gross misdemeanors and Category E felonies; 5 years for Category B, C, and D felonies; 7 years for misdemeanor DUI and domestic battery; and 10 years for Category A felonies.

Are there records that can never be sealed?

Yes. Felony sexual offenses, felony DUI cases, serious violent crimes, and crimes against children cannot be sealed under Nevada law. Most other offenses — including misdemeanors, non-violent felonies, and dismissed charges — are eligible. Fill out the form for a free eligibility check on your specific record.

What's the difference between record sealing and expungement?

Nevada doesn't have expungement — it has record sealing, which achieves a very similar result. A sealed record is hidden from public view and most background checks, effectively giving you a clean slate for employment and housing purposes.

Do I have to go to court?

A hearing may be required in some cases, but Attorney Stover handles all filings and court appearances on your behalf. We manage the entire process — from obtaining your criminal history from the Nevada Department of Public Safety to notifying all relevant agencies after the order is granted.

Will my record be completely invisible after sealing?

Sealed records are hidden from the general public, most employers, and landlords. Certain government agencies and law enforcement can still access them in limited circumstances. We'll explain exactly what sealing means for your specific situation.

Can I say I have no criminal record after sealing?

In most cases, yes — Nevada law allows you to answer "no" on job applications and housing forms that ask about criminal history, once your record is sealed. There are limited exceptions, such as applications for certain government jobs or professional licenses. We'll walk you through exactly what you can and cannot say.

Will my employer find out I'm sealing my record?

No. The process is confidential. Your employer is not notified, and once the record is sealed, it will not appear on standard background checks employers use.

Does Nevada have expungement?

Nevada does not have expungement, but record sealing achieves a very similar result. Your record is hidden from public view, most employers, and landlords — giving you a genuine fresh start. If you've been searching for "Nevada expungement," record sealing is what you're looking for.

Can I seal my record if I have cases in multiple Nevada counties?

Yes. We handle cases across all Nevada counties and have appeared in over 70 Nevada courts. Having records in multiple counties adds some complexity but is very manageable — we coordinate everything on your behalf.
